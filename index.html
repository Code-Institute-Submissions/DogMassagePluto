Stress Reduction

Stressed dogs is one of the main causes of physical ailments, anxiety and aggression.
Every situation can be stressful for your dog: a walk in the park with strange dogs, a visit to the vet, firework etc.
And the loss of a family member, move houses or relocation can be stressful for some dogs.
Some dogs can't keep the emotions in control.
Massage through stress reduction techniques will help.
These are common techniques that put the superficial tissues of the body in motion.
This allows more relaxation in the tissues.
It also has a strong effect on the nervous system and works with more body awareness and the physiological balance in the body.

Sport Massage

Sporting with your dog is poplar. Besides the usual sports like agility and flyball are many new sites in emerging like boot camp, anicross, balance and coordination and walking with your dog.
Running with cold muscles is disastrous, even if you are recreational sporting with your dog. The tissue of a weak muscle can not recover well. This can lead to pain, stiffness, muscle tension and muscle pavements.
Muscles can perform within a second, the blood flow will start after a few minutes. After about 15 minutes the blood circulation is optimal and the active muscles have enough oxygen to perform the properly.

Overstretching the muscles can lead to acidification of the muscle fiber. The recovery of overstrechted muscles takes about four to seven days, depending on the impact. Massage can help the body to dispose the resulting waste products appropriately. Through massage you give an external stimulus to the muscle. The warmth of your hand and the pressure can stimulate the circulation and encourages the lymphatic system.
If you massage the dog, impulses will go to the consciousness part of the brains, this makes the dog aware of his body. After sporting, cooling down is just as important. Walk easily for about five to ten minutes.

In this consult, we work on your problem-focused question on support of pain, physical problems and / or rehabilitation.

About Me

As a little girl I knew I wanted to work with animals.
When I was about 10 years old I started working at dog kennels in my holidays, a first step in learning how to work with dogs. In 2010 I received my degree in animalmanagement and I started to work as a dog trainer, where I still work with lots of joy. In 2013 I received my degree in dog sport massage and canine massage therapist.

Now I'm working 2 days a week as a dog trainer and 3 days a week as a dog massage therapist.
As a dog masseur I notice that the behavior and health of the dog grows every session.
I enjoy it when i see that the bond between the owner and the dog grows. It is a very beautiful and rewarding profession.
